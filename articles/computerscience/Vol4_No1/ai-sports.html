Artificial Intelligence in Sports

Daniel Gabai

Sports have remained unchanged for decades or even centuries. Although rule changes may occur, games are still fundamentally played the same way. Now, however, new technology is revolutionizing our approach to sports and it’s raising the level of play to unprecedented heights. Everything from a player’s fitness to refereeing and predicting games is being boosted with the power of artificial intelligence (AI) technology.

Fitness

Before an athlete even steps onto the field, they have already been training for possibly weeks or months. For an athlete, every small health detail matters; they need to be performing their best and therefore they need to feel their best. One of the best ways athletes in the modern day improve their fitness and overall performance is by using 3D inertial sensors and trackers to analyze every movement they make. This can allow them and their team to work on every small movement and fine-tune it perfectly. For example, IMeasureU is a company that develops a large array of sensors and trackers for different needs. Some of their uses include helping injured players return to their sport, preventing injuries in the future, and capturing precise movement which can be further analyzed. After this data is captured, it can be thoroughly inspected by professionals to determine any inefficiencies in an athlete’s movement which might be harmful in the long run. Not only can this data prevent and help treat injuries, but it can also instruct the athlete on the next steps in improving their game. One possible way to do this is to feed the data into AI virtual analysts in order to automatically show key weaknesses and strengths. Furthermore, AI is also able to instruct on the best way to become stronger and faster by analyzing a large group of similar athletes. This is one of the best aspects of this new technology, its ability to turn a large data set into understandable and potentially game-changing information. All of this happens way before game day and it is one of the reasons why athletes are so much faster and stronger today than they were 100 years ago.

Game Predictions

Ever wonder how sports networks on TV are able to display predictions for the outcome of the upcoming game? While the athletes are training with their virtual assistants, computers are going through and analyzing almost every previous game played. By factoring in every statistic available, from previous match-ups to measurable player statistics such as height, weight, speed, and likelihood of injury, algorithms can predict which team is more likely to win before the game has even started. Although many factors are at play during any sports game, from the players’ moods to the wind, an algorithm is able to predict with a high degree of accuracy regardless. For instance, in NFL games, AI algorithms were able to predict outcomes with a success rate of over 75%, significantly better than the 60% most experts in the sport can manage. One way they do this is by going through every game a certain team has ever played. For example, if team A beats team B and team C and both team B and team C beat team D, then the algorithm will likely predict that team A has a high chance of beating team D. Another important statistic to consider is what players are actually playing. A player who is injured or simply not playing may have a decisive impact on the outcome of the game. Finally, the thing that sets AI apart from any other computer program is its ability to learn from itself. One of AI’s best ways of improving its predictions is by running massive simulations against itself and determining its own factors which it considers to be important. All in all, when one is able to examine every possible data point involved, they are going to be better than someone going off intuition and surface observations.

Refereeing

One of the most practical uses of AI has actually been in refereeing sports games. In many sports, things simply play out too fast for most humans to comprehend. Although in-person referees are usually the ones who will initially make the calls, if a more in-depth examination is needed, special computer systems will need to be used. While the game is going on, high-speed cameras watch every play. Although for most sports a simple camera is enough, some sports need the power of AI in an attempt to make the fairest judgments possible. For example, in tennis, when a relatively small ball can be moving at speeds over 100 mph, humans can often misjudge where the ball landed because of factors like speed, wind, and air density. Most high-level tennis tournaments end up employing a system known as Hawkeye (also used in several other sports, most notably, cricket). Hawkeye employs advanced AI models to construct an almost perfect 3D model of the ball’s flight path. Afterward, it can analyze this in order to show where the ball landed within 0.04 inches.

Conclusion

Ultimately, the addition of AI-powered technology in sports has proved to be an incredibly useful asset for athletes and the entire industry. AI can improve every aspect of an athlete’s performance, well-being, and more. It can analyze every second of a game to determine the best strategies. AI can also predict the outcome of future games while refereeing present events. Ultimately, AI has positively changed sports forever and it will continue to improve the level of play as our technology improves.
