The Effects of AI on Jobs

Aditya Anand

Spring 2022

When one thinks of the future, they may envision a world run by robots, where humans can relax and sit back while an android does their chores for them. However, studies show that this may become a reality sooner than one may expect.

Past: History of Robots/AI

The Beginning

Artificial Intelligence is defined as “the ability of a computer or a robot controlled by a computer to do tasks that are usually done by humans.” The idea of robots was first presented in 1920 by Karel Capek, in his play, Rossum’s Universal Robots. The play presented robots taking over the world, giving birth to the science fiction genre.

The first robots were displayed in 1939 in the World’s Fair by the Westinghouse Electric Company. “Elektro the Moto-Man” had the ability to walk, talk, smoke cigarettes, blow up balloons, and move his head and arms. Elektro appeared again in 1940’s World Fair, along with Sparko, a robot dog who could sit, bark, and beg.

Following the revolutionary creations of Elektro and Sparko, Isaac Asimov created the three laws of robotics:

  1. A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.

  2. A robot must obey orders given to it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.

  3. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

These laws showed that robotics was beginning to be recognized as a prominent field of science.

Artificial Intelligence

While Elektro and Sparko were advanced for their time, they required voice commands to function. The first official artificial intelligence came in the form of two robot tortoises, Elmer and Elsie. Built by William Grey Walter in the late 1940s, these autonomous robots utilized sensory inputs by following light and using a bump sensor. These advancements developed not only technology, but also our understanding of the human nervous system.

Present: Modern Advancements

Horses

Many people question the fact that robots can take humans’ jobs. However, machines have already made one animal obsolete: the horse.

As the graph above depicts, the population of horses peaked in 1915 but sharply declined soon after. This is directly linked to the fact that cars began to be commonly used in the 1920s. As cars became more available, horses became unnecessary. Other jobs of horses were also impacted. Technology started to replace horses in transportation, agriculture, warfare, and more.

Current Robots/AI Replacing Jobs:

While one may think this won’t happen to humans any time soon, there are already intelligent robots amongst us. An example of this is Baxter, a robot with vision who can “learn what you want him to do by watching you do it.” Baxter costs about $25,000, which is about $40,000 less than the average household income in America. While the robot takes much more time performing tasks than a human, it is also much more cost effective.

This may sound like a new or complex idea, but there are already examples of artificial intelligence that replace human jobs. In new supermarkets, robot cashiers have replaced humans. “What used to be 30 humans is now 1 human overseeing 30 cashier robots.” Another example is robot baristas. Over five hundred thousand Americans work as baristas, and their job is in danger of being replaced by artificial intelligence.

Other fields are also impacted by robots. Chatbots now lead customer service and can help users do anything, from opening an account to transferring money. In addition, it is only a matter of time before self-driving cars, or autos, replace human drivers. The transportation industry currently consists of at least 70 million workers worldwide. Autos will replace these human drivers just as cars replaced horses. Ultimately, robots’ ability to think quickly with low chance of error allows them to take human jobs.

Professional Bots

Careers such as lawyers and doctors require years of education and hours of dedicated work every day. However, lawyers and doctors are still humans, and to err is human. For example, lawyers have to look through countless legal documents to find details supporting their case. Missing just one paragraph can severely increase their workload. Professional bots can look through millions of emails or documents in a matter of hours, whereas it would take humans weeks. Robots outweigh humans in cost, time, and accuracy.

Artificial intelligence can also leave a mark on the medical industry. Humans are not capable enough to understand all medicines and their interactions with each other. By analysing symptoms and comparing them to a database, robots can accurately diagnose patients and prescribe them the necessary drugs. While it is impossible for a human to know how every medicine affects the human body, this is not an issue for robots. Bots can also use the experience of other robots to improve themselves.

Watson, a robot created by IBM, provides guidance for lung cancer treatments at Memorial Sloan Kettering Medical Center. Watson is an example of how artificial intelligence can perform human tasks more efficiently. Watson has also made a guest appearance on ‘Jeopardy!’, where it showcased its talent.

While lawyers and doctors will most likely not become obsolete, their need and numbers will decrease.

Self-Teaching Bots

Breakthroughs in technology have even produced self-teaching robots. This may sound preposterous, but these bots are essential to America’s economy. The stock market, which used to be run by humans, is now run by “bots who taught themselves to trade stocks trading stocks” with other such bots. This is a matter of concern to human workers, as it shows the capability of artificial intelligence to learn and replace most if not all jobs.

Future: Paradise or Dystopia?

Long term effects of AI on Jobs

Robots already play a significant role in our economy. As they begin to take more jobs from people, humanity will be impacted. People may believe that technology will simply make life easier and allow humans to relax. However, when cars started to replace horses, people probably thought the same about them. Now we can see how negatively technology affected them, and how they can do the same to us.

There are many debates about whether or not AI will replace all jobs. Some argue that bots can teach themselves to do any work, while others say that careers which involve emotions, such as therapists, are safe. Nevertheless, most agree that robots can and will replace most jobs. Since AI is more efficient than humans, the economy will likely grow. However, humans will find themselves increasingly out of work.

Proposed Solution

The coming wave of AI is undeniable. The only action humanity can take is prepare for it. Several politicians agree that the best way to help the citizens is a Universal Basic Income, or UBI. A UBI is when the government pays a set amount of money to all citizens. This will help families sustain themselves even when their job is replaced by AI. However, many people are opposed to this idea, believing that it will make people lazy and overdependent on the government.

Conclusion

Robots will replace humans; it is not a matter of if, but rather, when. Politicians must work together and agree on a way to protect the citizens. If they don’t, people may find their own creations decreasing humanity’s population.
