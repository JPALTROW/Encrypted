Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare

Anna Shrestha

What is AI?

AI is using computer intelligence to imitate human thoughts and actions. Currently, there are four main fields of AI: reactive AI, limited memory AI, theory of mind AI, and self-aware AI. Of course, some of these have not been achieved and are merely theories and goals to achieve for the future.

The first field, and perhaps considered the simplest form of AI, is the reactive AI. Their outputs are calculated by deterministic algorithms. They cannot learn, nor can they change or grow unless explicitly expressed by the code. It is the most basic AI, but it is reactive AI that has led to further AI developments.

The next field is limited memory AI, which grows through the accumulation of data records. Through learning from these new records, they can perform more complicated tasks. It is also the type of AI that is most commonly used today. But like the name implies, the memory is limited and is only remembered for a short period of time.

The third type, the theory of mind AI, is just a theory as the name suggests. It’s considered an interactive AI which seems like a human, but not yet a human with emotions. It is a very close imitation of a human, and it can learn from their environment. Scientists are currently working on this field, and despite the difficulty of simulating emotional changes and facial expressions in conversation, there is progress is being made.

The last and most advanced kind of AI is self-aware AIAI which has its own emotions, almost like an artificial human. No self-aware AI has been developed, and this is purely in theory. The theory of mind AI would be a very close imitation that doesn’t understand why they do what they do and self-aware AI would be an artificial being.

Developments of AI Applications in Medicine

AI is faster and more accurate than humans in analyzing medical data and with more and more developments in AI, they have been put to practical use. It has had an especially important impact in medicine and healthcare, especially with the spread of COVID-19 in the past year and a half. The industry continues to grow, predicted to have a 41.7% compound annual rate of growth and reach $13 billion by 2025 from $1.2 billion in 2018. Investments are pouring in, and demand is predicted to grow to make up for unmet demands.

Drug Development and Research

AI models helped analyze COVID-19 data, managing to shorten the time needed for vaccine development from decades to merely months. It takes around 12 years of development, from the beginning of research to the vaccine reaching the patient, and only 5 in 5,000 drugs reach the stage of clinical trialsonly one of these five will be approved to be applied to humans. It also takes about $359 million just to develop one drug. AIs can streamline drug developments, reducing both the time and costs needed to develop a drug. For instance, in the development of genetic medicine, AI can analyze various genetic combinations to predict the possible effects of treatment. It is also essentially fair and objective, without being biased. This removes variables caused by differences in race or gender in clinical trials. This bias, which is unavoidable in humans, can also be controlled with changes to the hyperparameters to the desired degree (both with or without bias) in order to see the impact of race or gender in an experiment.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

NLP refers to the use AI in understanding and expressing human language, and it is used in text or sound recognition and translations. It is able to translate both human speaking and writing habits, and even ideas and emotions. Some AI have visual understanding through images. This allows patients to be taken care of in their most familiar language, or help doctors understand patients. It is also used to understand and sort medical documents, being used to analyze how to shorten the time required to improve medical methods and with better results. It is much faster and more accurate, being able to analyze more data and quickly give results when finding similar records. NLP allows doctors to convert clinical notes to EHR (electronic health records), so they only need to enter data once.

Rule-based Expert Systems

Rule-based expert systems rely on a large database of rules and information in order to rationalize certain specialized knowledge, but they can easily fail when the number of rules becomes too much. In addition, these rules are hard to change when the knowledge area is drastically revised and requires a vast database. Without the relevant knowledge, they cannot function. Rule-based expert systems can be considered an early form of AIs, but now more advanced AIs are being used to replace rule-based expert systems.

Early Detection and Diagnosis

AI is used for the diagnosis of diseases in early stages. For instance, it is used for mammograms. One in two healthy women get false results of having cancer with a normal diagnosis, but with AI, the analysis of mammograms is almost 30 times faster and has a 99% accuracy rate. It is both faster and more accurate. Another example is an AI tool that can predict AKI (acute kidney injury) almost 48 hours in advance it is able to find over 90% of AKI cases two days before normal diagnoses. MIT has also created an AI program that can analyze 3D images up to 1,000 times faster than humans. Wearable medical devices also help with early diagnosis as well as keeping in health, being able to monitor daily. With simple data analysis, healthcare providers can give early diagnoses of diseases and more appropriate cures. With simply a selfie, AI can find one’s chance of getting cancer by observing bilirubin levels in the white part of one’s pupils and comparing it with previous data. It can save thousands from cancer by detecting it earlier. The large amount of data allows AI to diagnose diseases through commonalities. This is faster and more accurate than normal doctors and even lowers costs. Through a diagnosis, AIs can also give accurate and optimal prescriptions.

Treatment

Robots with basic AI have been in use for more than 30 years, performing simple tasks to complex surgeries both assisting a doctor or alone. For instance, they can analyze previous records to optimize a surgerythis can lead to a 21% reduction in the patient’s stay duration. One study has shown that AI-assisted surgeries led to five times less issues compared to the surgeons working by themselves. They are much more accurate and have less mistakes than with a normal doctor working alone. They’re also being used for repetitive tasks, taking care of patients for rehabilitation, physical therapy, or long-term diseases. They can help care for the elderly, keeping them mentally healthy through conversation and care. AI also helps with the analysis of treatments through clinical notes and images, as it helps doctors find essential parts of medical imaging more accurately and in a faster time. A large database can allow for AI to detect diseases and prescribe treatments on their own, especially with the previous data of the patient. For instance, the AI Corti can help doctors during times of emergency by analyzing aspects of the phone call (the caller’s voice, background noises, past medical history) to detect heart attacks. It grows through more cases, and it is constantly improving. It has a 93% accuracy, compared to 73% accuracy in human predictions. A study in 2019 showed that AI was more effective than humans in identifying heart attacks. Virtual assistants are also a big help in the industry, alone reducing losses by $20 billion every year. They can be used anytime to answer questions, observe patients, and respond quickly. It can reduce unnecessary visits to the doctor.

Training

AIs allow for a more realistic simulation of certain conditions, which basic computer programs cannot do. Along with developments in NLP, AIs allow for a larger range of questions as well as targeted question sets. It is also much more convenient, being able to be accessed on most electronic devices.

Conclusion

Overall, AI has been playing a big role in various aspects of medicine and healthcare, including but not limited to decision making, research and development, training, diagnosis, and treatment. More is expected in the future from AI, including helping develop the next generation of radiology tools, expanding healthcare to regions that are developing or with limited access, decreasing the difficulties of EHRs, making antibiotic resistance safer by containing possible risks, showing more accurate analyses of pathology images, boosting the efficiency of medical devices by giving them intelligence, advancing immunotherapy for cancer treatment, creating a reliable risk detector, and observing health through wearable devices or smartphones.
